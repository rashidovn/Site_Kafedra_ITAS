Baccalaureate

Degree (qualification) of a graduate – the first degree at the university is a Bachelor of Science in Engineering and Technologies.

The Information-calculating techniques undergraduate course normally takes 4 years of full-time study.

Professional skill description of a graduate

After graduating from the university a Bachelor of Science in direction «Information-calculating techniques» can take up the posts of:

  • an engineer;
  • a programmer-engineer (a programmer);
  • an electronic-engineer (an electronic);
  • an automated control systems engineer;

and other posts in accordance with the graduate's professional skill.

Professional occupation field

Informatics and computer engineering – is a brunch of science and technology that combines ways and methods of man's activity aimed at development and use of:

  • Computers, systems and networks;
  • automated system for data-processing and control;
  • automated design systems;
  • software support for computer techniques and automated systems.

Professional occupation objects

Professional occupation objects for a Bachelor of Science in direction «Information-calculating techniques» are:

  • Computers, complexes, systems and networks;
  • automated system for data-processing and control;
  • automated design systems;
  • software support for computer techniques and automated systems (programs and software systems);
  • mathematical, informational, technical, ergonomic, organizational and legal support for the above-listed systems.

Kinds of professional occupation

After four years of university standard and specialized training a Bachelor of Science in direction «Information-calculating techniques» can do the following kinds of activities:

  • development effort;
  • industrial and technical activities;
  • research work;
  • management activities;
  • operating activity.

Kinds of professional occupation are determined by the university standard and industrial training programs that are worked out by the university.

    Requirements for a Bachelor's Training

    A Bachelor of Science in information-calculating techniques has to know:

    • new trends of development in information-calculating techniques and computing machinery. He must know how to use the theory in research engineering, development effort and in industrial and management activities;
    • standards and recommended practices of designing and professional occupation objects;
    • models, methods of analysis and engineering environment of mathematical, linguistic and informational software of calculating and automated systems;
    • basic operation principles of interface with user;
    • methods of analysis, research and simulation of computing and informational processes, which are connected operation of professional occupation objects and object elements;
    • in the process of development of computer systems, complexes and networks a Bachelor has to know programming principles, methods and ways of software and hardware interconnecting;
    • models, design methods and forms of development of professional occupation objects;
    • information security methods and engineered safety features of professional occupation objects;
    • order, methods and security facilities of intellectual property;
    • economic-organizing and legal regulations for industrial engineering and research investigations;
    • occupation safety and health regulations.

    A Bachelor ought to use his knowledge of:

    • methods and ways of development of requirements and specifications of professional occupation objects;
    • methods and development technique of professional occupation objects;
    • methods and engineering environment of mathematical, linguistic, informational and software support of calculating and automated systems;
    • test methods and testing aids of professional occupation objects;
    • methods of analysis and modeling aids of professional occupation objects and object elements;
    • modern information technologies and toolkit for solving different professional problems;
    • methods of design of professional occupation objects.
